About the Company

Date of Founding: PIKA. Game Dev Studio was officially founded on 18 May 2024 by Kislay Niraj. Unlike conventional startups, PIKA. began as a dream — not with capital or structure, but with a deep conviction that games can have soul.

This founding date now marks a turning point — a decision to step away from commercial noise and into a space where meaningful connection comes first.

Mission of the Company: Creating Games With Soul

PIKA.’s mission is to make games that carry a soul — designed not to impress, but to connect. Every game is a reflection of the creator’s emotional reality and is made to resonate long after the screen turns off.

Vision of the Company: Redefining Meaningful Gaming

PIKA. envisions a world where games are not only played but remembered. It aims to change the narrative from quick trends to timeless meaning — where stories become companions, and silence is as important as sound.

From artistic titles to ambient storytelling, PIKA. promises one thing — if a game doesn’t have a soul, it won’t be made.

About the Founder

Kislay Niraj is the quiet visionary behind PIKA. Game Dev Studio — an emotional creator in a results-driven world. He isn’t led by trends or analytics but by clarity, theme, and deep human moments. His creations explore topics like grief, time, memory, and self-worth — expressed not through dialogue but interaction.

He is a builder and a poet, a storyteller and a philosopher. Every frame, sound, and pixel is a reflection of his own journey — one that values authenticity over aesthetics, and connection over performance.

The Soul Behind PIKA.

Kislay Niraj began alone — no investors, no office, no shortcuts. What he had was something more powerful: purpose. Born in India and shaped by introspection, Kislay turned to game development as a way of giving voice to his unspoken world. It was never about success — it was about honesty.

Today, whether working with AI storytelling or building immersive indie experiences, Kislay remains grounded — crafting games not as a CEO, but as a human who wants players to feel truly seen.
